Portable Power Across Industries

Professional power tools, medical devices, and portable defense systems represent some of the most demanding portable battery applications. A defibrillator, an infusion pump, or a soldier's communications radio must perform reliably — with no margin for failure. Supply chain traceability for battery components is increasingly required by FDA quality system regulations and DoD procurement standards.

The supply chain visibility gap: Most portable battery products on the American market today contain cells manufactured in China or South Korea from minerals with no verified origin documentation. For medical devices and defense applications, this is not just a commercial concern — it is a compliance failure.

Key Supply Chain Requirements

  • Cylindrical or pouch cells with full supply chain documentation
  • NMC or LFP chemistry with IEC 62133 / UL 2054 certification
  • FDA 21 CFR 820 quality system compliance for medical applications
  • NDAA-compliant sourcing documentation for defense procurement
