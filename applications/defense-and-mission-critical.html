The Battery as a National Security Asset

Modern defense and national security operations are fundamentally dependent on reliable, high-performance energy storage. From soldier-portable systems to unmanned aerial vehicles, from forward operating base microgrids to secure communications infrastructure — batteries are as mission-critical as any weapons system.

Digital DNA Traceability

C4V's Digital DNA technology creates an immutable record of every material's origin and processing history from mine to finished cell. This enables defense procurement officers to verify, with cryptographic certainty, that a battery's lithium came from Nevada, its graphite from Alaska, its copper from Arizona — not from Chinese mines or processing facilities.

NDAA Section 841: Recent NDAA provisions restrict DoD procurement of batteries containing materials from covered entities linked to China. The American Energy maps supply chain configurations designed to be NDAA-compliant from day one.

Key Supply Chain Requirements

  • All minerals sourced from US or Five Eyes allied nations
  • Digital DNA cryptographic traceability from mine to finished cell
  • NDAA Section 841 compliance verification and documentation
  • MIL-STD-810 and MIL-PRF certified system integration options
